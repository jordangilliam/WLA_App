## 🏞️ Major Lakes & Reservoirs

### 1. **Lake Wallenpaupack** (5,700 acres) - Pike/Wayne Counties
   - PA's 3rd largest lake
   - Premier multi-species fishing destination
   - 52 miles of shoreline
   - Multiple boat launches and recreation areas
   - **Species**: Bass, Walleye, Perch, Pickerel, Panfish, Trout
   - **Facilities**: Boat launches, beaches, camping, marinas

### 2. **Promised Land Lake** (422 acres) - Pike County
   - Promised Land State Park
   - Natural glacial lake
   - Electric motors only - peaceful setting
   - Heavy trout stocking spring & fall
   - **Facilities**: Beach, camping, hiking trails (25+ miles), environmental programs

### 3. **Beltzville Lake** (949 acres) - Carbon County
   - Federal reservoir
   - Excellent bass, walleye, striped bass
   - Deep, clear water
   - **Facilities**: Swimming beach, multiple boat launches

### 4. **Gouldsboro Lake** (250 acres) - Wayne County
   - Gouldsboro State Park
   - Natural lake with trout and bass
   - Electric motors only
   - **Facilities**: Beach, camping, trails

### 5. **Tobyhanna Lake** (170 acres) - Monroe County
   - Tobyhanna State Park
   - Good trout and bass fishing
   - Popular camping destination
   - **Facilities**: Beach, camping, extensive hiking, winter sports

### 6. **Mauch Chunk Lake** (344 acres) - Carbon County
   - Near Jim Thorpe
   - Excellent bass and pickerel
   - Gasoline motors allowed
   - **Facilities**: Beach, camping, playground

## 🎣 Premier Trout Streams

### **Brodhead Creek** (25+ miles) - Monroe County
- **Premier Pocono Stream**
- Delayed Harvest section (catch & release, artificial lures only)
- Heavily stocked - tourist friendly
- Excellent fly fishing
- Green Drake hatches in late May
- **Access**: Brodhead Creek Heritage Trail, multiple parking areas
- **Species**: Brown Trout, Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass

### **McMichaels Creek** (15 miles) - Monroe County
- Stocked sections near Stroudsburg
- Wild trout in upper reaches
- Scenic setting
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow, Brook Trout

### **Tobyhanna Creek** (20 miles) - Monroe County
- Flows through Tobyhanna State Park
- Cold water holds trout well
- Spring and fall stocking
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow, Brook Trout

### **Big Bushkill Creek** (25 miles) - Pike County
- Delaware Water Gap area
- Some wild trout sections
- Scenic waterfalls
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow, Brook Trout, Smallmouth Bass

### **Lackawaxen River** (35 miles) - Pike/Wayne Counties
- Major tributary of Delaware River
- Excellent trout and bass
- Famous Zane Grey connection
- **Access**: Hawley, Lackawaxen town
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye

### **Pohopoco Creek** (20 miles) - Carbon/Monroe Counties
- Beltzville State Park area
- Spring stocking
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow Trout

### **Aquashicola Creek** (18 miles) - Carbon County
- Near Palmerton and Jim Thorpe
- Stocked sections
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow Trout

### **Shohola Creek** (15 miles) - Pike County
- Remote wild trout water
- Scenic setting
- **Species**: Brook Trout, Brown Trout, Rainbow Trout

### **Dyberry Creek** (15 miles) - Wayne County
- Flows through Honesdale
- Spring and fall stocking
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow Trout

### **Lehigh River (Upper Section)** (40 miles) - Carbon/Luzerne Counties
- Near White Haven and Jim Thorpe
- Whitewater sections
- Trout and bass
- **Species**: Brown, Rainbow Trout, Smallmouth Bass, Walleye

- Dingmans Falls

## 🏛️ Historic Sites & Attractions (Family-Friendly)

### 1. **Bushkill Falls** - "Niagara of Pennsylvania"
   - **Location**: Bushkill, Pike County
   - 8 waterfalls accessible by trails
   - Educational signage
   - 300+ acres
   - **Ecology**: Hemlock ravine ecosystem, waterfall formation
   - **Wildlife**: Brook trout, salamanders, forest birds

### 2. **Quiet Valley Living Historical Farm**
   - **Location**: Stroudsburg
   - **Date**: 1765 Pennsylvania German farm
   - Living history demonstrations
   - Traditional farming methods
   - Heritage animals and gardens
   - **Programs**: School tours, seasonal events
   - **Education**: Relationship between farming and nature

### 3. **Jim Thorpe Historic District**
   - **Location**: Jim Thorpe (former Mauch Chunk), Carbon County
   - Victorian-era architecture
   - Historic coal mining town
   - **Attractions**: Museums, scenic railroad, shopping
   - **Outdoor Access**: Gateway to Lehigh Gorge State Park
   - **History**: Industrial revolution, coal mining heritage
